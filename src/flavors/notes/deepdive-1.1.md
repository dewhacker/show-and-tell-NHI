
Voyager 1 was about 6.4 billion kilometers (4 billion miles) away, and approximately 32 degrees above the ecliptic plane, when it captured this portrait of our world. Caught in the center of scattered light rays (a result of taking the picture so close to the Sun), Earth appears as a tiny point of light, a crescent only 0.12 pixel in size. (further out than Pluto)

Launched 1977, photo taken 1990 → 13 years outbound before it looked back
5.5 hours for the signal to reach Earth at light speed
~2.5 months for the full image data to actually arrive
