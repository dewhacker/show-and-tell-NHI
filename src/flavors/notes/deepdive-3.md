Lightspeed

~300,000 km/s (186,000 mi/s) — the universe's hard speed limit
Nothing with mass can reach it; takes infinite energy to try
Light from the Sun takes 8 min 20 sec to reach Earth — when you see the sun, you're seeing the past
Proxima Centauri (nearest star) is 4.24 light years away — the light hitting your eye from it left in ~2022

Einstein's Twin Thought Experiment (1905, special relativity)

Take identical twins. One stays on Earth, one boards a near-lightspeed ship
Time dilation: the faster you move through space, the slower you move through time (relative to a stationary observer)
When traveler returns, they're younger than the twin who stayed
Not science fiction — confirmed experimentally with atomic clocks on planes (Hafele-Keating, 1971), and GPS satellites must correct for it daily or your maps would drift miles off
The faster you go, the more extreme the effect. At 99% lightspeed, time slows by ~7x. At 99.9%, ~22x. At 99.99%, ~70x

Round trip to Proxima Centauri (4.24 ly each way, 8.48 ly total)

Returns to a sibling 7+ years older
